# Dashboard Story

## Storyline Goal
Move from **“How much did we sell?”** to **“Which customer behaviors create sustainable value, and what should we do next?”**

## Visual-by-Visual Narrative

### 1) KPI Cards
**What it shows**
- Average LTV
- Total Customers
- Average Repeat LTV
- Repeat Customer %

**Why it matters**
This top row gives leaders a fast health-check on customer value and retention quality. In particular, the gap between Average Repeat LTV and Average LTV provides a direct signal of retention upside.

---

### 2) Top 20 Customers by LTV (Horizontal Bar)
**What it shows**
- Ranked high-value customers by total revenue contribution.

**Why it matters**
Highlights revenue concentration and identifies accounts that may require proactive protection (VIP service, outreach cadence, tailored offers).

---

### 3) Order Frequency vs Average Order Value (Scatter)
**What it shows**
- Each point is a customer.
- X-axis: Order Frequency
- Y-axis: Average Order Value
- Annotations: high-value, high-AOV, frequent customer clusters.

**Why it matters**
Separates customer value drivers into behavior patterns. This enables targeted actions:
- Frequent but lower-AOV customers → upsell/cross-sell focus.
- High-AOV but low-frequency customers → retention cadence + premium experience.

---

### 4) LTV Distribution (Binned)
**What it shows**
- Number of customers in each LTV value range.

**Why it matters**
Typically reveals a right-skewed pattern: a broad base of low-to-mid LTV customers and a long tail of high-value customers. This supports prioritization between scale programs and VIP protection.

---

### 5) Invoice Year Filter (2010/2011)
**What it shows**
- Year-level comparison across all visuals.

**Why it matters**
Allows quick diagnostic checks on whether value patterns are stable over time or shifting year-to-year.

## Executive Message
1. Repeat customers are materially more valuable than the average customer.
2. High-value customers are a smaller segment with outsized revenue impact.
3. Customer value is not one-dimensional: both frequency and AOV matter.
4. Retention and VIP strategy should be first-priority growth levers.
